jemand (someone)

Explanation

Function:
Refers to an unspecified person; used when the identity is unknown or irrelevant.

Sentence structure:
Subject or object in a sentence, acts like a pronoun.

Notes:
Can be used in nominative, accusative, and dative depending on context.

Examples

  • Jemand hat an der Tür geklopft.
    (Someone knocked on the door.)
  • Ich kenne jemanden, der dir helfen kann.
    (I know someone who can help you.)
  • Gibt es hier jemanden zu sprechen?
    (Is there someone here to talk to?)
  • Jemandem wurde das Auto gestohlen.
    (Someone had their car stolen.)
  • Ich habe jemandem eine Nachricht hinterlassen.
    (I left a message for someone.)

niemand (no one)

Explanation

Function:
Denies existence of any person; no person at all.

Sentence structure:
Used as subject or object, declines like jemand.

Notes:
Often used to express negation in sentences.

Examples

  • Niemand war zu Hause.
    (No one was at home.)
  • Ich kenne niemanden in dieser Stadt.
    (I know no one in this city.)
  • Niemandem wurde geholfen.
    (No one was helped.)
  • Niemand versteht das Problem.
    (No one understands the problem.)
  • Hier war niemand gestern.
    (No one was here yesterday.)

jeder (everyone)

Explanation

Function:
Refers to every person individually in a group.

Sentence structure:
Usually singular, acts like a pronoun.

Notes:
Declines according to case; often followed by singular verb.

Examples

  • Jeder hat das Buch gelesen.
    (Everyone read the book.)
  • Ich kenne jeden hier.
    (I know everyone here.)
  • Jeder bekommt ein Geschenk.
    (Everyone gets a gift.)
  • Hat jeder das verstanden?
    (Did everyone understand that?)
  • Jeder soll pünktlich sein.
    (Everyone should be punctual.)

alle (all)

Explanation

Function:
Refers to the entire group collectively.

Sentence structure:
Usually plural, verb agrees in plural.

Notes:
Different from "jeder" which refers individually.

Examples

    (All have come.)
  • Ich kenne alle Schüler.
    (I know all the students.)
  • Alle lieben Schokolade.
    (Everyone loves chocolate.)
  • Alle müssen helfen.
    (Everyone must help.)
  • Wir haben alle gewonnen.
    (We all won.)

man (one/people)

Explanation

Function:
Impersonal pronoun, used to express general statements.

Sentence structure:
Always singular verb, no explicit subject.

Notes:
Equivalent to English “one” or “people” in general.

Examples

  • Man sagt, dass das Wetter schön wird.
    (People say the weather will be nice.)
  • Man darf hier nicht rauchen.
    (One is not allowed to smoke here.)
  • Man braucht Geduld.
    (One needs patience.)
  • Man kann viel lernen.
    (One can learn a lot.)
  • Man sagt, es sei so.
    (People say it is so.)
